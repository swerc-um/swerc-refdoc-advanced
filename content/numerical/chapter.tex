\section{Polynomials and recurrences}

	\textbf{Linear recurrences}\\
	\[
X \cdot
\begin{bmatrix}
f(i)\\[4pt]
f(i+1)\\[4pt]
\vdots\\[4pt]
f(i+k-1)
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
f(i+1)\\[4pt]
f(i+2)\\[4pt]
\vdots\\[4pt]
f(i+k)
\end{bmatrix}.
\]
\[
X =
\begin{bmatrix}
0 & 1 & \cdots & 0\\[4pt]
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\[4pt]
0 & 0 & \cdots & 1\\[4pt]
c_k & c_{k-1} & \cdots & c_1
\end{bmatrix}.
\]

In the first \(k-1\) rows, each element is \(0\) except that one element is \(1\).
These rows replace \(f(i)\) with \(f(i+1)\), \(f(i+1)\) with \(f(i+2)\), and so on.
The last row contains the coefficients of the recurrence to calculate the new value \(f(i+k)\).
